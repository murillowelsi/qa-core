# Reflections on Testing

## Thoughtful Questions for Growth

Use this space to explore your testing philosophy and practices.

---

## Core Principles Reflection

### Testing Shows Presence of Defects

- How has this principle changed how you approach testing?
- Where do you find the most valuable defects?
- What does "success" mean for your testing?

### Exhaustive Testing is Impossible

- How do you prioritize when you can't test everything?
- What criteria do you use to make trade-offs?
- How do stakeholders respond to "we can't test 100%"?

### Early Testing

- How early in your projects do you start testing?
- What barriers prevent earlier testing?
- What's the biggest win from early testing you've seen?

### Defect Clustering

- Where are the bugs in your software?
- How does past data guide your testing focus?
- Do you use risk-based testing?

### Pesticide Paradox

- How do you keep tests fresh and relevant?
- When do tests stop finding new issues?
- How often do you review and update tests?

### Testing is Context Dependent

- What's unique about testing in your domain?
- How do you adapt standard practices to your context?
- What works vs what doesn't in your environment?

### Absence of Errors Fallacy

- What does "quality" mean for your product?
- What happens after you find no bugs?
- How do you validate user satisfaction?

## Remember

Good testing is about:

- **Understanding principles** - Not just techniques
- **Adapting to context** - No one-size-fits-all
- **Continuous learning** - Testing evolves
- **Team collaboration** - Quality is everyone's job
- **Measurable improvement** - Data-driven decisions
- **User focus** - Testing is about user value
